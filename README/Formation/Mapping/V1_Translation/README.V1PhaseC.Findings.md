## Surviving Laws From Intact Seams

- Preserve source continuity through pre-ingest normalization. Raw sample/value streams may be buffered, timestamped, chunked, copied, and normalized into one lawful raw input shape without transforming content or claiming artifact authority.
- Keep executive handoff narrow. The runtime entry seam may validate and forward one lawful raw shape, but it must not attach interpretive, review, or promotion surfaces at handoff.
- Allow deterministic primary transformation while keeping provenance explicit. `A1`, `A2`, and `W1[]` may be emitted through ingest, alignment, and windowing only if stream identity, policy chain, time/grid coordinates, and input lineage remain reconstructable end to end.
- The first support attachment may remain lawful when the primary object stays class-stable. `H1` may be committed into substrate support paths while anomaly reports, segment transitions, and substrate bookkeeping stay explicitly subordinate and non-authoritative.
- Basin and query support surfaces may survive if their claim ceilings remain explicit. Basin/query outputs may read committed state and emit observational or tooling surfaces only, without mutating stored artifacts or claiming truth, identity, memory closure, or canon.
- Direct read-side consumers may format without recomputing authority. A terminal HUD may copy and render lawful runtime or workbench sections as-is, but display formatting must not become a new authority seam.

## Forbidden Moves From Mixed Or Quarantine Seams

- Do not emit primary continuation output and replay/reconstruction output from one convenience seam without an explicit class split. `M1[]` and `A3` sharing one bounded seam increases class-mixing pressure even when both are labeled honestly.
- Do not co-locate primary artifacts, support summaries, and interpretive overlays inside one composite result object with compatibility aliases. Explicit labels help, but packaging all three classes into one convenience bundle still raises overread risk.
- Do not make cross-run support accumulation depend on inherited interpretive overlays. Comparative support seams should not require attention/memory overlay presence in order to admit or compare runs.
- Do not attach readiness, canon-candidate, and consensus-review packaging as ordinary runtime integration furniture. Promotion-boundary material must stay more sharply separated than a convenient workbench bundle with mirrored alias bridges.
- Do not hide projection identity behind alias fallback chains or synthetic projection IDs. Read-side projection may compress for inspection, but it must not weaken direct source traceability through fallback-derived labels.
- Do not attach canon-adjacent or Door Two consultation material as ordinary neighboring HUD furniture. Precomputed consultation records belong behind an explicit downstream boundary, not as casual adjacent display near V1 structural inspection.

## Do / Don't Translation List

### Do

- Do preserve the raw-input normalization and executive handoff pattern as one narrow lower-primary continuity family.
- Do preserve deterministic primary transformation seams only where provenance, policy lineage, and non-claims stay explicit.
- Do preserve the first primary/support commit pattern only when support bookkeeping remains clearly secondary to the emitted primary object.
- Do preserve basin/query support seams only as bounded observational or tooling surfaces with explicit claim ceilings.
- Do preserve direct read-side formatting consumers only when they consume upstream output as-is and do not synthesize new authority.

### Don't

- Don't port mixed packaging objects that bundle primary, support, and interpretive surfaces for convenience.
- Don't port replay/reconstruction output inside the same seam as primary continuation without a sharper split.
- Don't port cross-run seams that inherit interpretive dependence as an admission requirement.
- Don't port workbench-style readiness/review/canon-boundary adjacency as a default runtime exposure pattern.
- Don't port projection seams that rely on synthetic IDs, alias fallback, or canon-adjacent attachments to stay legible.

## Seam-Family Findings

- Lower primary continuity seams are the strongest carry-forward family. The input boundary, executive handoff, and `3a` all preserve deterministic transformation, narrow scope, and reconstructable provenance without class inflation.
- Lawful support attachment seams can survive when they stay visibly subordinate. `3b` and `3d` show that support can attach to primary flow without becoming identity, truth, memory, or canon authority if the seam keeps claim ceilings and non-claims explicit.
- Mixed co-location seams are the recurring structural risk. `3c`, `Result assembly seam`, and `Workbench integration seam` all remain honest enough to study, but each packages more than one class family in one bounded convenience seam.
- Projection synthesis seams are where traceability starts to soften. `7a` remains clean because it formats directly, while `7b` becomes mixed because it synthesizes a new projection model through alias readers and fallback identity shaping.
- Boundary-adjacent consultation and canon-pressure seams should be treated as highest-friction downstream attachments. `7c` is useful as evidence, but its adjacency to structural HUD inspection makes it quarantine-worthy rather than a safe default V2 carry-forward pattern.

## Translation Posture Summary

- Carry-forward worthy: raw-input normalization, executive handoff, deterministic alignment/window emission, first subordinate primary/support commit, bounded basin/query support surfaces, and direct formatting-only HUD consumption.
- Re-derive worthy: merge/reconstruct packaging, composite result assembly, cross-run accumulation, workbench integration, and structural HUD projection. These seams retain useful intent, but their class boundaries should be restated in smaller and cleaner V2 surfaces.
- Quarantine-worthy: consultation attachment of precomputed canon-adjacent / Door Two material beside V1 structural HUD inspection. Keep it as evidence of downstream pressure, not as a default adjacency pattern for V2.
