## 1. Why This Note Exists

DME already has meaningful bounded controls for weak or unstable conditions:

- anomaly / divergence behavior
- retained-tier insufficiency
- threshold / downgrade posture
- replay / reconstruction failure visibility
- structural identity weakening
- distortion audit pressure

But these are not yet unified under one explicit accounting surface.

As the system grows more operator-facing and replay / reconstruction become more legible, a recurring drift risk becomes stronger:

noise, uncertainty, distortion, insufficiency, ambiguity, and degradation can be loosely blended into one vague "low confidence" story.

That is not lawful enough for DME.

This note exists to prevent that collapse.

Its core role is:

- preserve the mathematical origin of weak-state conditions
- preserve bounded interpretive vocabulary
- prevent semantic reading from outranking structural support

## 2. Core Rule

In DME, noise, uncertainty, and distortion are not interchangeable. They arise at different places, mean different things, and justify different downstream postures.

Corollary rules:

- source variability is not the same as claim uncertainty
- distortion is not the same as insufficiency
- unresolved posture is not the same as degradation
- semantic ambiguity must not replace structural accounting
- operator-facing interpretation must remain traceable back to bounded structural support

One-line summary:

Noise is a property of the source / support path, uncertainty is a property of bounded claim legitimacy at the current seam, and distortion is a property of representation collapse that risks misleading later interpretation.

## 4. The Key Distinctions

### 4.1 Noise

Noise in DME is variability or disturbance in the source or support path.

Noise may arise from:

- measurement contamination
- environmental perturbation
- source instability
- injected synthetic variability
- sampling irregularity
- transform sensitivity
- support degradation

Noise is a property of the signal / support pathway, not by itself a claim-status judgment.

Noise may be:

- low
- moderate
- high
- structured
- unstructured
- transient
- persistent

Noise does not automatically mean:

- uncertainty
- insufficiency
- distortion
- rupture
- failed replay legitimacy

Noise is an input-side or support-path condition.

### 4.2 Distortion

Distortion in DME is representation collapse, transformation loss, or simplification strong enough to risk misleading later interpretation.

Distortion may arise from:

- compression
- retention loss
- summary collapse
- over-coarse read-side mapping
- flattening of distinct cases into one story
- aggressive transformation or reminting
- replay / reconstruction overstatement

Distortion is not just "difference."
It is difference that matters because it weakens interpretive legitimacy.

A structure can remain useful while distorted.
But if distortion crosses the relevant boundary, identity language, replay language, or continuity language must weaken.

Distortion is a representation-level condition.

### 4.3 Uncertainty

Uncertainty in DME is bounded inability to justify a stronger claim at the current seam.

Uncertainty arises when the system cannot lawfully determine a stronger posture because:

- support is incomplete
- multiple plausible readings remain
- structural evidence is too weak
- retained tier is too weak
- mechanized basis is incomplete
- current distinctions are insufficient for a stronger bounded answer

Uncertainty is therefore a claim-status condition, not merely a source condition.

Uncertainty does not mean the system is broken.
It means the stronger claim is not currently justified.

### 4.4 Insufficiency

Insufficiency is a specific bounded case of uncertainty:

there is not enough surviving support for the bounded question to be answered strongly at the current seam.

Examples:

- retained tier too weak
- replay basis too weak
- reconstruction basis too weak
- lineage too weak
- required distinctions do not survive

Insufficiency is not the same as degradation.
Insufficiency means the support floor for the claim is not met.

### 4.5 Degradation

Degradation means weaker-but-still-supported posture.

A degraded state still preserves some bounded support and may still justify a weaker claim, replay, or reconstruction posture.

Degradation is not:

- strong conservation
- full insufficiency
- total failure

It is a weaker supported state.

### 4.6 Unresolved

Unresolved means the system cannot honestly justify either stronger continuity or stronger break.

Unresolved posture is lawful.

It should be preferred over:

- overconfident continuity
- overconfident rupture
- decorative failure softening
- fake equivalence

Unresolved is a legitimate bounded output class.

### 4.7 Rupture

Rupture is a structural break or discontinuity significant enough that prior retained support or continuity language no longer governs strongly.

Rupture is not just noise.
Rupture is not just uncertainty.
Rupture is a bounded continuity break.

### 4.8 Ambiguity

Ambiguity is an operator-facing or interpretive condition where multiple nearby readings remain legible and uncollapsed.

Ambiguity may reflect:

- real uncertainty
- weak support
- coarse representation
- overlapping structure
- unresolved continuity

Ambiguity should not be treated as a replacement term for the underlying condition.
It is usually a display / reading symptom, not the primary accounting class.

## 5. Where These Enter the Pipeline

### 5.1 Source Layer

At the source layer, the main accounting classes are:

- source variability
- perturbation
- measurement instability
- synthetic noise parameters
- source-family differences

This is where noise most directly appears.

### 5.2 Structural Derivation Layer

At the structural derivation layer, the main accounting classes are:

- divergence
- concentration changes
- redistribution
- anomaly emergence
- transition instability
- recurrence weakening

This is where noise becomes structurally visible, but still not yet a claim-status posture.

### 5.3 Retention / Compression Layer

At the retention / compression layer, the main accounting classes are:

- support survival
- loss
- distortion
- compression degradation
- retained-tier weakening
- rebuildability limits

This is where distortion becomes especially important.

### 5.4 Replay / Reconstruction Layer

At the replay / reconstruction layer, the main accounting classes are:

- fidelity posture
- threshold posture
- downgrade posture
- insufficiency
- unresolved
- failed
- reconstructable_only
- replay_not_justified

This is where uncertainty becomes operationally central.

### 5.5 Operator / Interpretation Layer

At the operator / read-side layer, the main accounting classes are:

- what is clearly supported
- what is weakly supported
- what is degraded
- what is insufficient
- what remains unresolved
- what distinctions are visible
- what remains blurred

This is where structural conditions become bounded semantic posture.

The rule here is:

interpretation must remain traceable back to the structural and mechanized basis that produced it.

## 6. Mathematical Basis vs Semantic Layer

DME begins from mathematical operators and structural transforms.

That remains true.

Interpretive vocabulary appears later because the operator needs bounded language for:

- continuity
- drift
- rupture
- recurrence
- insufficiency
- unresolved posture
- replay legitimacy
- reconstruction quality

This is lawful only while:

- the semantic layer remains lower-authority than the structural layer
- the semantic layer is traceable to the mathematical support path
- no semantic summary outruns the support basis that produced it

So the governing rule is:

DME does not move away from mathematics when interpretation appears; it only becomes risky when semantic language stops being accountable to structural support.

This note exists partly to prevent that drift.

## 7. Weak-State Lattice

For practical use, DME should treat weak-state accounting as a lattice rather than one generic confidence score.

Useful classes include:

- noise_present
- structured_variability
- distorted
- degraded
- insufficient_support
- unresolved
- failed
- rupture
- recurrence
- review_required

Not every seam needs every class.
But the system should avoid collapsing them into one "low confidence" bucket.

## 8. Lawful Downgrade Relation

The preferred downgrade relations are:

noise may lead to:

- weaker structure visibility
- weaker replay quality
- uncertainty
- but not automatically insufficiency

distortion may lead to:

- degraded identity
- degraded replay
- insufficient support
- misleading similarity if not accounted for

uncertainty may lead to:

- unresolved posture
- review_required
- bounded caution

insufficiency should lead to:

- retained_tier_insufficient
- replay_not_justified
- reconstructable_only
- review_required

rupture should lead to:

- continuity break
- new identity required
- not vague similarity comfort

## 9. What This Note Prevents

This note is meant to prevent several recurring failures:

### 9.1 Noise Collapse

Treating every weak or unstable condition as "noise."

### 9.2 Confidence Collapse

Treating all weak-state conditions as one generic confidence number.

### 9.3 Semantic Inflation

Using richer language than the structural basis justifies.

### 9.4 Distortion Blindness

Failing to distinguish real structural change from representation collapse.

### 9.5 Uncertainty Evasion

Forcing crisp claims when unresolved posture is more lawful.

## 11. Minimal Operator Questions

A good operator / accounting surface should eventually help answer:

- is the current weak state mostly source noise, support weakness, distortion, or claim uncertainty?
- is continuity weakening, broken, unresolved, or merely noisy?
- what kind of support still survives?
- what can still be replayed or reconstructed honestly?
- what is no longer justified?
- what next action is lawful?

If these cannot be answered, the accounting remains too collapsed.
