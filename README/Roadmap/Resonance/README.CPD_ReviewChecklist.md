# CPD Review Checklist

## Status

**Status:** Draft operator checklist
**Role:** Practical review aid
**Placement intent:** `Roadmap/DoorTwo/Resonance/`
**Purpose:** Provide a repeatable checklist for reviewing Consensus Pressure Descriptors (CPDs) so they remain lawful, bounded, and useful without drifting into authority, settlement, or canon behavior.

---

## Purpose

This checklist operationalizes the following documents:

* **Resonance–DME Integration Boundary**
* **Consensus Pressure Descriptor Specification**
* **Resonance Vocabulary Translation Table**

Its job is simple:

> Help an operator determine whether a CPD is clear, bounded, challengeable, and lawful to use in review.

A CPD should pass through this checklist before it is:

* attached to a candidate packet,
* cited in review,
* surfaced in a UI,
* reused across comparison families,
* treated as “stabilized” support.

---

## Core Reminder

Before using this checklist, remember:

* A CPD is **not canon**.
* A CPD is **not settlement**.
* A CPD is **not a truth claim**.
* A CPD may **inform** review, but cannot **settle** review.

If the surrounding workflow forgets that, stop and correct posture first.

---

## Quick Pass / Fail Screen

A CPD should **not** proceed if any of the following are missing:

* clearly identified subject,
* declared lens,
* non-authoritative support posture,
* inflation risk statement,
* at least one challenge hook,
* explicit allowed use,
* explicit disallowed use.

If any of these are absent, the CPD remains incomplete.

---

## Section 1 — Subject Clarity

### 1.1 What exactly is being described?

* Is the subject clearly named?
* Is it obvious whether the CPD concerns a candidate object, transition, basin relation, replay pattern, or comparison?
* Could a second reader identify the same subject without guesswork?

### 1.2 Is the scope narrow enough?

* Is the descriptor attached to a specific object or bounded family?
* Has the CPD avoided broadening from “this case” to “the general truth”?
* Is the comparison set explicit if the CPD is comparative?

### Pass condition

The reader can answer: **what is this CPD about?** in one sentence.

---

## Section 2 — Lens Discipline

### 2.1 Is the lens declared?

* Is the interpretive lens named explicitly?
* Does the CPD make clear whether it is harmonic, field, replay-based, basin-based, comparative, or mixed?

### 2.2 Is the lens bounded?

* Is it clear what this lens includes?
* Is it clear what this lens does **not** claim?
* Would a lens change plausibly alter the CPD outcome?

### 2.3 Has lens-dependence been preserved in wording?

* Does the CPD say “under this lens” instead of sounding universal?
* Has the wording avoided turning lens-specific fit into global fit?

### Pass condition

The reader can answer: **under what declared perspective does this CPD hold?**

---

## Section 3 — Translation Hygiene

### 3.1 Are high-risk Resonance terms translated safely?

Check especially for:

* coherence
* spectral state
* phase lock
* attractor basin
* symmetry preservation
* cavity mode fit
* spectral identity
* resonant consensus

### 3.2 Does the wording preserve mechanism while avoiding authority leakage?

* Is “coherence” translated as support or convergence pressure?
* Is “phase lock” translated as alignment stability under declared lens?
* Is “attractor basin” translated as stable support neighborhood or convergence basin?
* Is “resonant consensus” treated as a consensus-pressure model, not a result?

### 3.3 Has the descriptor avoided importing ontology silently?

* Does the CPD avoid phrases like “is the true state,” “proves the object,” or “settles identity”?

### Pass condition

The CPD uses Resonance vocabulary in a way that is **narrow, translated, and non-self-authorizing**.

---

## Section 4 — Support Surface Quality

### 4.1 Are the support surfaces present?

Review whether the CPD meaningfully addresses applicable surfaces such as:

* harmonic compatibility support
* phase alignment support
* basin convergence support
* interference amplification support
* symmetry preservation support
* boundary-fit support
* coherence decay risk

### 4.2 Are the surfaces decomposed rather than collapsed?

* Can the reader inspect the individual support surfaces?
* Has the CPD avoided hiding everything inside one aggregate score?

### 4.3 Are the support surfaces interpretable?

* Are numeric scales declared?
* Are categories explained?
* Is there enough narrative context to understand what the numbers mean?

### Pass condition

The reader can answer: **what kinds of support pressure are actually being observed here?**

---

## Section 5 — Inflation Risk Review

### 5.1 Is inflation risk stated explicitly?

* Has the author declared whether inflation risk is low, moderate, or high?
* Is the stated inflation risk believable?

### 5.2 Are the likely overreads named?

Examples:

* coherence being mistaken for truth,
* fit being mistaken for admissibility,
* identity-support being mistaken for identity proof,
* cavity-specific stability being mistaken for generality.

### 5.3 Does the CPD visually or rhetorically overstate itself?

* Does the language sound too conclusive?
* Does the layout imply a ranking of truth?
* Would a casual reader overestimate what the CPD establishes?

### Pass condition

The CPD actively names its own overread risk instead of hiding it.

---

## Section 6 — Challengeability

### 6.1 Are there real challenge hooks?

* Does the CPD state how it can be contested?
* Are those challenge hooks concrete rather than ceremonial?

Examples:

* change the lens,
* broaden the comparison family,
* vary replay tier,
* test cross-run stability,
* test scale sensitivity,
* compare against alternate basin family.

### 6.2 Could a reviewer actually perform the challenge?

* Are the hooks operationally meaningful?
* Are they specific enough to guide next action?

### 6.3 Is there any counterevidence noted?

* Does the CPD acknowledge tensions, contradictions, or fragility?
* If not, is it suspiciously one-sided?

### Pass condition

The reader can answer: **how would I try to break this descriptor?**

---

## Section 7 — Boundary-Fit and Generality

### 7.1 Is the CPD broad or cavity-specific?

* Does the support appear only under narrow boundary conditions?
* Is cavity-specificity or lens sensitivity explicitly stated?

### 7.2 Has the CPD confused local fit with general fit?

* Is the descriptor clear about whether the pattern is local, provisional, or broad?
* Is any cross-run or cross-scale stability actually evidenced?

### 7.3 Does the CPD identify the conditions under which support decays?

* What happens when the replay question changes?
* What happens when retention tier changes?
* What happens when the comparison family widens?

### Pass condition

The CPD is honest about **where** it works and where it probably does not.

---

## Section 8 — Conservation and Distinctions

### 8.1 What distinctions appear preserved?

* Does the CPD name what remains stable across transforms?
* Are those distinctions actually relevant to the candidate or family?

### 8.2 What distinctions may have been compressed away?

* Has the CPD asked whether compactness is hiding important structure?
* Is there any risk that “stability” came from flattening rather than preserving?

### 8.3 Is symmetry language being used carefully?

* Is “symmetry preservation” functioning as a support descriptor rather than a proof?

### Pass condition

The CPD distinguishes between **preserved structure** and **over-compressed structure**.

---

## Section 9 — Authority Leakage Check

### 9.1 Does the CPD sound like a decision?

Scan for wording like:

* “therefore valid,”
* “therefore true,”
* “therefore should be promoted,”
* “settled,”
* “confirmed,”
* “canonical.”

### 9.2 Is the CPD being used as a hidden threshold gate?

* Are people informally treating high support as acceptance?
* Are advisory scores acting like hard criteria?

### 9.3 Is the CPD being mistaken for minting readiness?

* Is a support artifact quietly behaving like a candidate packet verdict?

### Pass condition

The CPD remains visibly support-only and does not function as disguised authority.

---

## Section 10 — Present Allowed / Disallowed Use

### 10.1 Is allowed use explicit?

Examples:

* support appendix for candidate review,
* comparison aid,
* probe-side descriptor,
* challenge input,
* interpretation surface.

### 10.2 Is disallowed use explicit?

Examples:

* not for promotion,
* not for canon justification,
* not for settlement,
* not for UI confidence ranking,
* not for runtime override.

### 10.3 Does current usage match the declaration?

* Is the artifact actually being used in a lawful way right now?

### Pass condition

There is no ambiguity about what the CPD may and may not currently do.

---

## Section 11 — Lifecycle Readiness

### 11.1 Is the status appropriate?

Suggested statuses:

* draft
* reviewed
* contested
* stabilized
* retired
* superseded

### 11.2 Has the CPD earned a stronger status?

* Was it reviewed properly?
* Has it survived comparison, challenge, and re-reading?
* Is “stabilized” being used too early?

### 11.3 Is retirement or supersession needed?

* Is the descriptor obsolete?
* Has a clearer descriptor replaced it?
* Has later evidence made it misleading?

### Pass condition

The lifecycle status matches actual review maturity.

---

## Section 12 — Candidate Packet Attachment Check

Use this section only if the CPD is being attached to a candidate packet.

### 12.1 Is the CPD clearly labeled as appendix/support?

* Is it visibly subordinate to packet review rather than replacing it?

### 12.2 Does the packet avoid overclaiming from the CPD?

* Is the CPD cited as one support family among others?
* Has the packet avoided phrases like “the CPD proves legitimacy”?

### 12.3 Are promotion blockers still visible?

* Has the CPD clearly stated why it cannot directly justify activation or minting?

### Pass condition

The CPD functions as **supportive context**, not disguised verdict.

---

## Review Outcome Categories

After using the checklist, assign one of the following outcomes:

### A. Pass — Lawful Support Artifact

The CPD is bounded, clear, challengeable, and safe to use as support.

### B. Pass with Revisions

The CPD is basically lawful, but requires wording fixes, added challenge hooks, clearer lens declaration, or stronger inflation warnings.

### C. Hold — Too Ambiguous

The CPD is too unclear, too broad, too translated poorly, or too under-specified to use reliably.

### D. Reject — Authority Leakage Risk

The CPD is behaving like hidden settlement, ontology import, or minting pressure and must not proceed in current form.

### E. Retire / Supersede

The CPD has been outgrown, invalidated, or replaced by a stronger bounded descriptor.

---

## Short-Form Operator Checklist

For quick use, ask:

1. What exactly is this CPD about?
2. What declared lens is carrying it?
3. What is it actually claiming?
4. What is it **not** claiming?
5. What support surfaces are present?
6. What is the inflation risk?
7. How would I challenge it?
8. Is it cavity-specific or broad?
9. Is it being mistaken for authority?
10. Is its current use explicitly lawful?

If any of these cannot be answered quickly, the CPD needs revision.

---

## Red Flags

Immediately pause review if any of these appear:

* “coherence” used as if it means truth,
* “fit” used as if it means admissibility,
* missing declared lens,
* missing challenge hooks,
* hidden aggregate score,
* no inflation risk statement,
* overly universal wording,
* UI styling that implies settled ranking,
* candidate packet language that leans on CPD as verdict,
* unsupported claim that stability equals identity.

---

## One-Sentence Rule

> Review every CPD as a support artifact that must earn clarity, challengeability, and bounded usefulness without ever being allowed to impersonate a decision.

---

## Closing

This checklist is intentionally practical.

Its purpose is not to make CPDs bureaucratic.
Its purpose is to keep them **lawful while they are still useful**.

That is the only way coherence pressure can enter DME without prematurely becoming authority.
