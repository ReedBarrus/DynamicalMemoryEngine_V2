# Resonance–DME Integration Boundary

## Status

**Status:** Draft boundary note
**Role:** Integration and differentiation document
**Scope:** Defines how Resonance may inform Dynamical Memory Engine (DME) without collapsing DME’s authority structure, promotion discipline, or constitutional posture.

---

## Purpose

This document establishes the lawful boundary between **Resonance** and **Dynamical Memory Engine (DME)**.

The goal is **not** to merge the two systems wholesale. The goal is to define:

1. what Resonance contributes,
2. what DME does **not** inherit,
3. how Resonance concepts may be translated into DME-compatible forms,
4. where integration is permitted in the DME layer stack,
5. what must remain explicitly excluded until separately justified.

This boundary exists to preserve both:

* the compressive explanatory power of Resonance, and
* the epistemic discipline of DME.

In short:

> Resonance may supply candidate field-mechanism lenses.
> DME remains the authority discipline that governs when any such lens is merely interesting, structurally useful, review-relevant, promotion-eligible, or canon-worthy.

---

## Core Boundary Thesis

Resonance and DME are adjacent but non-identical systems.

### Resonance primarily contributes

* a **native field / spectral ontology**,
* a compact theory of structural agreement via **coherence**,
* a family of geometric / dynamical validation mechanisms,
* strong intuitions about compression, resonance, similarity, anomaly, and coordination pressure.

### DME primarily contributes

* **epistemic and governance discipline**,
* separation of measurement, structure, runtime memory, recognition, and promotion,
* explicit protection against authority bleed,
* challenge, contest, supersession, and lifecycle governance,
* bounded posture regarding ontology, truth, and canon.

Therefore:

> DME may use Resonance to model how structural agreement pressure behaves,
> but DME must not allow Resonance coherence claims to self-upgrade into truth, canon, or authority without passing through DME’s explicit review and promotion boundaries.

---

## Non-Negotiable DME Preservation Rules

Any integration with Resonance must preserve the following DME principles:

* **Runtime is not canon.**
* **Query is not truth.**
* **Substrate is not ontology.**
* **Consensus is promotion-only**, not runtime settlement.
* **Read surfaces are non-authority surfaces** unless explicitly promoted.
* **Candidate structure is not self-justifying** merely because it recurs, compresses well, or appears coherent.
* **Promotion remains challengeable** and subject to supersession.

These are not implementation preferences. They are constitutional protections.

If a proposed Resonance import weakens any of these, the import is out of bounds.

## What Resonance May Lawfully Contribute

The following imports are permitted **as candidate explanatory lenses, review-side descriptors, or probe-side support mechanisms**.

### 1. Coherence as structural pressure

Resonance offers a useful language for how candidate structures reinforce, align, decay, destabilize, or fail to fit.

Within DME, this may be used to describe:

* convergence pressure,
* recurrence fit,
* alignment pressure between candidate objects,
* reinforcement vs dissipation tendencies,
* selective stability under declared lens conditions.

**Allowed use:** support descriptor, analysis lens, review narrative, or quantitative probe metric.

**Disallowed use:** direct truth criterion, canon criterion, or autonomous promotion trigger.

---

### 2. Geometric consensus mechanisms as consensus-pressure lenses

Resonance’s six consensus mechanisms are particularly valuable as a vocabulary for **review-side legitimacy pressure**:

* harmonic compatibility,
* phase lock,
* attractor basin stability,
* constructive interference threshold,
* symmetry preservation,
* cavity mode fitting.

Within DME, these may be translated into bounded descriptors such as:

* `harmonic_compatibility_support`
* `phase_alignment_support`
* `basin_convergence_support`
* `interference_amplification_support`
* `symmetry_preservation_support`
* `boundary_fit_support`
* `coherence_decay_risk`

These descriptors are lawful **only if** they are:

* declared lens-bound,
* explicitly non-authoritative,
* reversible,
* challengeable,
* separable from canon status.

---

### 3. Spectral / field ontology as a candidate explanatory model

Resonance may be used as a candidate model for explaining why certain basin structures, transitions, recurrences, or support patterns appear in DME.

This includes candidate explanations for:

* why some basin neighborhoods remain stable,
* why some trajectories reinforce and others dissipate,
* why some recurrences behave like standing modes,
* why some transitions preserve recognizable identity support while others do not.

This remains an **interpretive model**, not an automatically inherited ontology.

**Allowed phrasing:**

* “Resonant-field ontology is a candidate explanation of this structural behavior.”
* “This basin pattern is consistent with a standing-mode interpretation under the declared lens.”

**Disallowed phrasing:**

* “This proves the basin is literally a resonant object.”
* “This confirms the ontology.”

---

### 4. Conservation / symmetry language in review and challenge

Resonance’s symmetry-preservation framing can strengthen DME’s review process.

Useful questions include:

* What distinctions must remain conserved for this candidate to remain legitimate?
* What symmetries does the candidate implicitly assume?
* What boundary conditions make the candidate appear valid?
* What breaks when those symmetry assumptions fail?
* Is stability broad or cavity-specific?

This language is especially helpful in:

* candidate packet review,
* challenge and contest workflows,
* supersession arguments,
* governability assessment.

---

### 5. Demonstration and pedagogy surfaces

Resonance visualizations, simulations, and demo surfaces may be used as **pedagogical or illustrative aids** to help humans understand candidate coherence mechanisms.

They may support:

* operator intuition,
* conceptual socialization,
* explanatory materials,
* comparative exploration.

They must remain explicitly labeled as:

* demonstration surfaces,
* interpretive aids,
* simulation surfaces,
* non-authoritative views.

They must not silently become evidence of promotion-worthiness by aesthetic force alone.

## What DME Must Not Inherit

The following Resonance claims are **not imported by default**.

### 1. “Spectral state is the data.”

DME must not inherit the claim that spectral state is intrinsically the primary or final data reality.

At most, DME may say:

> Spectral state is a privileged structural representation for certain families, lenses, or bounded questions.

This preserves empirical flexibility and avoids premature ontology lock-in.

---

### 2. “Coherence is consensus.”

DME must not equate coherence with settlement.

Coherence may contribute evidence of:

* structural fit,
* support pressure,
* convergence tendency,
* review relevance,
* legitimacy pressure.

But coherence does **not** equal:

* acceptance,
* truth,
* promotion,
* canon,
* authority.

DME consensus remains an explicit promotion boundary, not a runtime dynamical equivalence.

---

### 3. “Identity is spectral fingerprint.”

Resonance may treat identity as directly spectral.

DME must remain more cautious.

Within DME, spectral support may contribute to identity assessment, but identity remains conditional on:

* declared lens,
* retained tier,
* bounded replay question,
* continuity across relevant transformations,
* distortion tolerance,
* challenge outcome.

At most:

> A spectral fingerprint may serve as a provisional identity-support surface.

It must not be treated as unconditional identity ground.

---

### 4. “Geometry itself settles truth.”

DME must refuse any direct slide from elegant field behavior to epistemic closure.

Even if a mechanism is compelling, compressive, or physically suggestive, DME requires:

* provenance,
* declared observational posture,
* bounded question integrity,
* review surfaces,
* promotion criteria,
* challengeability.

This is precisely where DME acts as an epistemological testing engine.

---

### 5. Device / compute / speculative horizon imports into constitutional core

Resonance’s device, body-coupling, piezo-native sensing, and resonant-compute horizons are valuable as adjacent field maps and future exploratory directions.

They are **not** constitutional imports into DME’s current authority stack by default.

They may be:

* parked in adjacent horizon notes,
* explored in probe form,
* used as translation targets later,
* compared structurally to DME findings.

They are not presently suitable as substrate law or canon-bearing claims.

## Where Integration Is Allowed in the DME Staircase

### Allowed zones

Integration is currently allowed in:

* **interpretation surfaces**,
* **probe design**,
* **review descriptors**,
* **candidate packet support narratives**,
* **challenge / contest / supersession analysis**,
* **adjacent explanatory notes**.

### Restricted zones

Integration is restricted or prohibited in:

* ingest authority,
* runtime settlement,
* canon minting,
* automatic promotion rules,
* substrate ontology declarations,
* any path where coherence pressure silently becomes authority.

### Summary

Resonance may inform **how DME interprets pressure**.
It may not replace **how DME governs trust**.

## Review and Promotion Guardrails

Any Resonance-derived descriptor or interpretation must answer the following before it is allowed to influence promotion:

1. **What exactly is being claimed?**
   Mechanism? interpretation? ontology? identity? settlement pressure?

2. **What declared lens is carrying the claim?**
   Is the lens explicit, bounded, and challengeable?

3. **What is conserved across transforms?**
   Are we tracking real continuity, or merely aesthetic fit?

4. **What would falsify the claim?**
   If nothing would, it is not promotion-safe.

5. **What is the inflation risk?**
   Could this descriptor be overread into authority, truth, or canon?

6. **What challenge surface is available?**
   Can the claim be contested, compared, or superseded?

If these cannot be answered clearly, the Resonance-derived import remains below promotion.

## Why This Boundary Exists

Resonance is powerful because it compresses many phenomena into a compact field grammar.

DME is powerful because it refuses to let compactness masquerade as epistemic entitlement.

This boundary preserves both strengths.

Without Resonance, DME risks lacking a vivid field-level vocabulary for structural agreement pressure.

Without DME, Resonance risks allowing coherence, elegance, and ontology to collapse into one another too quickly.

The boundary therefore functions as a productive asymmetry:

* Resonance contributes candidate mechanism and field intuition.
* DME contributes epistemic discipline, promotion law, and challenge structure.

## One-Sentence Operating Rule

> DME may incorporate Resonance as a declared, non-authoritative field-mechanism lens for consensus pressure, while prohibiting any direct collapse from coherence into truth, authority, canon, or self-settling ontology.

Resonance is best treated inside DME as:

* a candidate field ontology,

The lawful bridge is therefore:

> Use Resonance to model the physics of agreement pressure.
> Use DME to govern the legitimacy of agreement claims.
