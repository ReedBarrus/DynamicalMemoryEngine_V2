## 1. Why this note exists

The Workflow Mechanization facet now has enough grammar and object types that one final coordination problem becomes real:

**how should the workflow judge repeated outcomes without collapsing local audit into premature authority, and without leaving long-horizon pressure purely conversational?**

Without an explicit audit/review note, several distortions become likely:

- local audits remain isolated and never accumulate into regime intelligence,
- repeated helper/tool failures remain anecdotal,
- repeated false readiness remains visible but not classed,
- different object classes receive the same flat review posture,
- stronger review appears ad hoc rather than earned,
- and audit summaries begin to feel authoritative without a declared review law.

This note exists to prevent that drift.

Audit is the accountability surface.
Review is the stronger bounded response to accumulated, justified pressure.

---

## 2. Core rule

**Audit records what is locally true, weak, distorted, drifting, or unresolved about a bounded workflow object. Review occurs only when local audit outcomes accumulate enough lawful pressure to justify stronger classification, rerouting, refinement, or escalation.**

Corollary rules:

- audit is not acceptance
- review is not truth
- repeated signals are not self-justifying authority
- regime accumulation is not promotion
- stronger review must be earned by bounded evidence
- unresolved posture is lawful when support is weak

The purpose is:
- preserve local honesty,
- preserve longitudinal learning,
- and avoid both underreaction and overclaim.

---

## 3. Audit posture

Audit is first-class in this facet.

Every bounded workflow object that moves meaningfully through the workflow should be auditable.

Audit asks, at minimum:

- what object is this?
- what was it supposed to preserve?
- what survived?
- what drifted?
- what was flattened or overclaimed?
- what is now true?
- what is still not claimed?
- what is the lawful next route?

Audit should remain:
- local,
- explicit,
- attributable,
- and subordinate to evidence.

Audit must not become:
- convenience narration,
- hidden approval,
- pseudo-promotion,
- or a substitute for seam evidence.

---

## 4. Audit telemetry families

The primary telemetry families remain:

- **distortion**
- **drift**
- **uncertainty**

These are the first diagnostic families across all bounded workflow objects. 

### 4.1 Distortion

Distortion is present when the workflow object or its summary compresses distinctions strongly enough that later interpretation becomes misleading.

Examples:
- scaffold presented as implementation
- request object presented as fulfillment
- helper summary presented as seam truth
- route pressure hidden under “advance”

### 4.2 Drift

Drift is present when the object migrates away from its declared question, role, authority posture, route class, or object family.

Examples:
- packet drift
- seam drift
- helper drift
- schema drift
- route drift

### 4.3 Uncertainty

Uncertainty is present when support is too weak to justify stronger advancement honestly.

Examples:
- unclear identity continuity
- unclear closure status
- unclear route class
- insufficient support for acceptance
- unresolved regime classification

Uncertainty is not a failure of the workflow.
It is the lawful posture when support is not strong enough.

---

## 5. Review posture

Review is stronger than audit.

Review occurs when repeated bounded audit outcomes create enough pressure that simple local routing is no longer sufficient.

Review may:
- reclassify an object,
- tighten its allowed use,
- demand fission,
- require mutation under authored oversight,
- harden a helper boundary,
- downgrade a stronger claim,
- or defer a family until more lawful support exists.

Review must remain:
- bounded,
- object-aware,
- evidence-aware,
- hierarchy-aware,
- and explicit about what it does **not** decide.

Review does **not** imply:
- canon,
- truth,
- architectural finality,
- or automatic merge.

---

## 6. Regime accumulation

A regime is a repeated outcome family across bounded audit events.

Regime accumulation exists so the workflow can learn from repeated patterns without converting repetition itself into authority.

Regimes should be recognized when:
- a similar audit signal recurs across one object family,
- one seam repeatedly exhibits the same pressure,
- one helper repeatedly succeeds or fails in the same bounded role,
- or one workflow path repeatedly produces the same lawful tension.

Regime accumulation should remain:
- evidence-backed,
- bounded,
- and revisable.

It should not become:
- mythology,
- permanent identity branding,
- or automatic policy.

---

## 7. First regime families

The following regime families are allowed in v0.

### 7.1 Stable advance

Repeated clean closure with preserved non-claims and low distortion.

### 7.2 Repair pressure

Repeated `return_for_repair` or repeated local insufficiency within the same object family.

### 7.3 Mutation pressure

Repeated signals that the object no longer preserves its current identity/role and needs authored redesign or split.

### 7.4 False readiness

Repeated tendency for declared/displayed/generated surfaces to be spoken of as more complete than they are.

### 7.5 Premature expansion

Repeated attempts to activate broader scope than current law or Door posture justifies.

### 7.6 Lane insufficiency

Repeated blockage caused by tool lane weakness, file-size fragility, validation weakness, or helper-capability limits.

### 7.7 Route oscillation

The object or family keeps bouncing among repair / defer / escalate / narrow without stabilizing.

### 7.8 Seam overmixing

Repeated evidence that one seam or packet is carrying too many distinct responsibilities.

### 7.9 Helper overreach

A helper repeatedly acts above declared support posture.

### 7.10 Helper stabilization

A helper repeatedly succeeds within one bounded lawful role strongly enough to justify a more explicit wrapper or tooling class.

## 8. Object classes under review

Different workflow object classes should be reviewed differently.

This matters because flat review posture causes distortion.

---

## 8.1 Development objects

Examples:
- packets
- seams
- closure-state objects
- audit objects
- regime records

Primary review questions:
- did the object preserve the same bounded question?
- did it preserve routeability?
- did it preserve authority posture?
- did it complete honestly?

Likely review actions:
- accept
- revise
- narrow
- split
- defer
- escalate

---

## 8.2 Agents and helper roles

Examples:
- helper classes
- wrappers
- retrieval helpers
- local-model wrappers
- registry helpers

Primary review questions:
- did the helper remain inside its declared role?
- did it preserve explicit non-claims?
- did it rely on lawful seam evidence?
- is it repeatedly overreaching or stabilizing?

Likely review actions:
- tighten posture
- reduce role
- split helper class
- formalize wrapper support
- defer broader helper growth

---

## 8.3 Schemas and schema families

Examples:
- README schema family
- packet schema family
- wrapper schema family
- registry schema family

Primary review questions:
- is the schema producing lawful object shells?
- is the schema family overgeneralizing?
- is schema convenience flattening real seam differences?
- is the family still narrow enough for admission honesty?

Likely review actions:
- keep family narrow
- split family
- deprecate family
- add stronger admission constraints

---

## 8.4 Stubs and scaffold objects

Examples:
- generated note shells
- packet shells
- seam-entry shells
- wrapper shells
- test shells

Primary review questions:
- is the stub clearly still a stub?
- did the stub preserve object identity?
- is it being overread as implementation?
- are explicit non-claims still visible?

Likely review actions:
- admit as scaffold
- return for repair
- mark superseded
- escalate for authored mutation

---

## 8.5 Emission/admission objects

Examples:
- scaffold emissions
- delta emissions
- request emissions
- admission outputs

Primary review questions:
- was the right object class emitted?
- did admission match actual status honestly?
- is there repeated mismatch between emission and lawful admission?
- are regimes accumulating around one emission family?

Likely review actions:
- tighten grammar
- split emission class
- add stronger admission tests
- defer one family

## 9. Audit output shape

A practical workflow audit should emit at minimum:

- `object_id`
- `object_class`
- `bounded_question`
- `audit_scope`
- `what_is_now_true`
- `what_is_still_not_claimed`
- `dominant_telemetry`
- `identity_posture`
- `closure_posture`
- `route_recommendation`
- `regime_signals_if_any`

Optional:
- `support_basis`
- `known_risks`
- `review_threshold_reached`
- `fission_pressure`
- `helper_posture_note`

This remains a workflow object, not runtime truth.

## 10. Review thresholds

Stronger review is justified when one or more of the following occur:

### 10.1 Repetition threshold
The same bounded failure or pressure recurs across enough cycles/packets that treating it as isolated becomes misleading.

### 10.2 Identity threshold
The object no longer clearly preserves the same bounded question, constraints, or role.

### 10.3 Distortion threshold
The object or its summaries are repeatedly flattening distinctions in ways that mislead downstream workflow.

### 10.4 Authority threshold
A helper, wrapper, note, or packet is beginning to absorb authority it was not granted.

### 10.5 Lane threshold
Current tools or seams repeatedly cannot complete lawful work without fission, manual rescue, or escalation.

When a threshold is crossed, stronger review is justified.
Not before.

## 12. Relationship to closure gate

The closure gate determines when a capability may be called mechanized.

Audit/review determines:
- whether that closure is being preserved honestly across workflow motion,
- whether repeated near-misses or distortions are accumulating,
- and whether object classes, helper classes, or schema families need tightening or splitting. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

So:
- closure gate is capability-status discipline
- audit/review is longitudinal workflow discipline

Both are required.

## 13. Relationship to structural identity

This note depends directly on structural identity law.

Review must never preserve object continuity language when:
- the bounded question has drifted,
- the declared constraints no longer hold,
- support survival is insufficient,
- or the basis is no longer mechanized enough for the current claim class. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

When that occurs:
- narrow,
- split,
- downgrade,
- or mark new identity required.

## 14. Relationship to distortion audit

This note composes directly with the Distortion Audit Protocol.

Distortion audit helps determine:
- what distinctions are collapsing,
- how severe the collapse is,
- and where refinement belongs. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Audit/review then determines:
- whether the workflow object should change class,
- whether the family needs stronger review,
- or whether ambiguity should remain visible.

So:
- distortion audit is a fidelity tool
- audit/review is a workflow response tool
